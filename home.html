Detailed Requirements

Click on a topic to expand and view the necessary documents and fees.

New Application Requirements:

  • Barangay Business Clearance (from your respective barangay)
  • DTI Registration (Business Name) or SEC Registration (Corporation)
  • Contract of Lease (if renting) or Tax Declaration (if owned)
  • Locational Clearance from Zoning Office
  • Sanitary Permit (from Municipal Health Office)
  • Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (BFP)
  • Environmental Compliance Certificate (if applicable)
  • Picture of Establishment (2x2 or actual photo)

Note: Fees vary depending on business type and capital. Please visit the Treasurer's Office for assessment.

File Application Form Online

Requirements:

  • Proof of Residency (Utility Bill, Rental Agreement)
  • Valid ID (Passport, Driver's License, Voter's ID)
  • Application Form (available at Barangay Hall)
  • Community Tax Certificate (Cedula) - sometimes required depending on purpose

Processing Time: Usually released on the same day or within 1-3 days depending on verification.

File Barangay Clearance Form

Requirements:

  • Application Forms (Signed & Sealed by Engineer/Architect)
  • Locational Clearance
  • Photocopy of Title / Tax Declaration
  • Photocopy of Current Tax Receipt
  • Site Development Plan
  • Building Plans (Floor Plans, Elevations, Sections)
  • Structural Analysis & Design (for 2-storey and up)
File Building Permit Application

Available Documents:

  • Birth Certificate (Late Registration available)
  • Marriage Certificate
  • Death Certificate
  • CENOMAR (Certificate of No Marriage) - *Note: Usually processed via PSA online, but local registry can assist.

Authorization: If requesting for someone else, bring an authorization letter and your ID.

General Filing Steps

A simplified overview of the process for new business applications.

1

Barangay Level

Secure Barangay Business Clearance and Barangay Clearance from your local Barangay Captain.

2

Zoning & Health

Obtain Locational Clearance (MPDC) and Health/Sanitary Permit (MHO).

3

Fire & Safety

Secure Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (FSIC) from the Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP) station.

4

Mayor's Office

Submit all clearances to the Business Permits & Licensing Office (BPLO) for the Mayor's Permit.
